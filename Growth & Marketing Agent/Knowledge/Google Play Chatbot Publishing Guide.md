# **A comprehensive, step-by-step guide to publishing AI chatbot apps on Google Play**

The growing proliferation of AI and large language models has fundamentally changed the landscape of mobile apps. AI chatbots, ranging from virtual assistants and customer service representatives to creative writing tools, are one of the fastest-growing categories in digital stores. However, publishing an AI chatbot on the Google Play platform requires navigating a highly regulated ecosystem. Google has strict guidelines for developer verification, data privacy, mandatory testing requirements, and, most importantly, specialized policies governing AI-generated content.1As a comprehensive reference document and practical guide, this report describes in detail, step by step, all the stages of deploying an AI chatbot, from the preparation stage and legal requirements to optimization and final deployment.

## **1\. Create a business identity and obtain a unique D-U-N-S number for the organizational account**

Before uploading any code or files to the platform, a developer must establish a legal and verified presence in the Google Play Developer Console. Google Play distinguishes between “Personal” and “Organization” developer accounts. For developers planning to release a commercial AI chatbot, registering as an organization is highly recommended. Organization accounts not only convey a more professional image to users, but also exempt developers from some of the more restrictive and time-consuming testing requirements imposed on personal accounts.3.  
To register an organizational account, the legal entity must have a D-U-N-S (Data Universal Numbering System) number.4This number is a unique nine-digit identifier assigned by Dun & Bradstreet and verifies the legal existence of a business globally.4The process of obtaining this number is free but processing may take up to 30 days, which requires prior planning before scheduling the release of the program.4A very critical point at this stage is that the legal name and address recorded in the Dun & Bradstreet profile must exactly match the information that is going to be entered in the Google Payments profile.4The slightest inconsistency in these details is a major factor in the failure of the developer account verification process.2.

## **2\. Verify the identity of your Google Play developer account and provide legal documents**

After receiving the DANCE number, the developer must create a Google Play Console account and go through rigorous identity verification checks. In July 2023, Google introduced new policies to increase transparency and combat fraud, requiring all developers to undergo rigorous identity verification.2.  
In this step, a Payments Profile must first be connected to the account. This profile is used to verify the organization's identity, even if the chatbot is completely free.2Organizations must upload their official business documents, such as a company registration certificate, articles of association, or value-added tax (VAT) registration certificate.2In addition to the corporate documents, an authorized representative of the organization (such as the CEO or legal representative) must provide personal and government-issued photo identification (such as a passport, national ID card, or driver's license).2To prevent requests from being rejected by Google's optical character recognition (OCR) systems, identity documents must be valid, in color, properly lit, and not copied.2Any tampering with the documents can lead to permanent account blocking and removal of all apps from the store.2.

## **3\. Configure Merchant Account for Monetization Strategies**

AI chatbots typically have high processing costs (due to the use of advanced APIs), so their monetization models are often based on monthly subscriptions (such as access to premium models such as GPT-4) or tokenized in-app purchases. To facilitate these transactions globally, a detailed business account configuration is required. The developer should go to the Financial Settings section of the Play Console and enter their detailed banking information that matches the country of registration of the organization.11.  
This business account is responsible for processing payments, deducting Google Play services fees, and depositing monthly earnings into the developer's bank account.11Tax regulations vary by developer region and the developer must complete the relevant tax forms (such as W-8BEN forms for non-US individuals) to avoid having chatbot earnings withheld.13.

## **4\. Strict compliance with policies governing AI-generated content**

Chatbots present unique regulatory challenges. Because these apps generate content dynamically based on user prompts, there is an inherent risk of producing harmful, deceptive, or offensive material. Google Play has dedicated a separate section to its “AI-Generated Content Policy” in its recent updates.14This policy explicitly states that text-based chatbot applications where interaction with artificial intelligence is the primary feature of the application are subject to these strict rules.15.  
The fundamental requirement in this step is that developers are ultimately responsible for the output of their AI programs.1. The chatbot must not produce any prohibited content. Prohibited items include producing non-consensual sexual images or texts (deepfakes), content that exploits children, content that encourages harmful behavior or self-harm, deceptive and fake election content, and creating malicious code or content that facilitates fraud.14To achieve this level of safety, developers must use advanced filters and classifiers to block harmful inputs and outputs. For example, if the chatbot uses OpenAI's API, the developer must implement the company's Moderation API endpoint to scan and block content if necessary before displaying a response to the user.1Google also encourages developers to use industry-standard frameworks such as the Google Secure AI Framework (SAIF) and the OWASP Top 10 Security Guide for LLM programs.1.

## **5\. Mandatory implementation of in-app reporting mechanisms and user monitoring**

To ensure ongoing compliance with Google Play policies, AI chatbot apps must have highly visible and accessible in-app reporting features.14This is not an optional requirement, but an inviolable rule for productive AI-based programs.  
The reporting mechanism should be designed so that users can easily flag or report offensive or inappropriate AI-generated content directly in the chat interface, without having to leave the app or visit an external website.14After a user files a report, the developer is legally and politically obligated to review these reports and use the feedback to continually improve their app's filtering and content moderation systems.16An accumulation of unhandled user reports of bots producing offensive content could lead to the app being quickly suspended by Google Play's moderation team.17.

## **6\. Develop a privacy policy and manage conversation data (GDPR and CCPA compliance)**

Chatbots process vast amounts of conversational data, often containing personally identifiable information (PII), financial information, or sensitive health details that users voluntarily enter into the system. Strict compliance with global privacy laws, including the European General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA), is absolutely essential at this stage.19.  
The developer must develop a comprehensive Privacy Policy document that is accessible both within the app and on the Google Play store page.21This document should clearly state what data is collected, what the legal basis is for processing this data, how long it is retained, and with which third parties the data is shared.19The Data Minimisation Principle in the GDPR requires that a chatbot only collects information that is strictly necessary for its function.19.  
Additionally, data sharing with LLM providers should be fully disclosed to users. For example, if a chatbot uses OpenAI’s API, although the company will no longer use the API data to train its models after March 2023, it will retain conversation history (including prompts and outputs) for abuse monitoring purposes for up to 30 days.22This 30-day retention by a third party is legally considered a form of data sharing and must be disclosed in the Google Play Privacy Policy and Data Security Form, unless the developer has received confirmation of a "Zero Data Retention" policy from the provider.20Finally, the app should provide a clear mechanism for users to completely delete their conversation history and account from the developer's servers.19.

## **7\. Adhering to strict technical specifications, target API levels, and file formatting**

The Google Play platform requires developers to keep their apps up to modern technical standards to ensure security, privacy, and optimal performance on newer Android devices.

* **Program package format:**The file submitted to Google Play must be in the Android App Bundle (AAB) format, and the use of the old APK format for new apps is deprecated. The AAB format allows Google Play to produce and distribute optimized, lighter versions of the app tailored to the hardware architecture and screen resolution of the user's device. The size limit for the base AAB file is usually 150 MB.  
* **Target API Level:**This is one of the most critical technical requirements. Google periodically increases the minimum target API level to ensure apps are compatible with the latest Android security features. For example, starting August 31, 2025, all new apps and updates must target at least API level 35 (equivalent to Android 15).24This requirement will be upgraded to API level 36 (equivalent to Android 16\) from August 31, 2026\.25Failure to meet these levels will cause the app to be hidden and unsearchable on devices using newer versions of Android.25.

| Technical and system requirements | Mandatory specifications for new applications | Reason for implementation and benefits |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Program distribution format** | Android App Bundle (.aab) | Optimized download size and better compatibility with a wide range of Android devices. |
| **Target API Level (2025)** | Minimum API Level 35 (Android 15\) | Compliance with modern security permissions and background management policies24. |
| **Target API Level (2026)** | Minimum API Level 36 (Android 16\) | Ensuring app visibility for new users with the latest operating system version25. |
| **Permissions** | Limited and Contextual | Prevent unnecessary permissions requests (such as precise location) that violate the principle of data minimization26. |

## **8\. Execute the closed testing phase and pass the mandatory testing requirements (for personal accounts)**

Google has radically changed the structure of app testing in an effort to purge the store of low-quality and unstable apps. The testing phase, which was previously an optional quality control option, has now become a legal and mandatory prerequisite for a certain group of developers. This rule applies specifically to developers who created their “Personal” account after November 13, 2023\.27This is why in the first step, creating an "Organizational" account was highly recommended, as organizational accounts are currently exempt from this time bottleneck.3.  
The mechanism of this closed testing is that the developer must recruit at least 12 testers who voluntarily enter the testing path of the app in the Google Play Console.28(It's worth noting that Google reduced this requirement from 20 testers to 12 in December 2024 due to pressure from the independent developer community, but kept the testing duration the same.)28These 12 testers must install the app on their devices and keep it active for at least 14 consecutive days.27.  
The most important challenge in this step is maintaining the 14-day continuity. If the number of active testers drops below 12 even for a day, the 14-day timer may reset and the developer will have to start the entire two-week process over again.28The optimal strategy for chatbots is to recruit at least 15-20 testers to create a safety margin against user attrition.28. Also, the actual interaction of these users with the chatbot is important; Google monitors usage patterns to ensure the legitimacy of the experiment. After the successful completion of this 14-day period, the developer must fill out a comprehensive questionnaire in the console, explaining the feedback received and the bugs fixed during this period, before the “Apply for production access” button is enabled for him.27.

## **9\. Compile optimized metadata (ASO) with strict adherence to fraud and copyright policies**

After overcoming the technical and testing hurdles, the focus should shift to App Store Optimization (ASO). This step involves preparing the text and information that the user sees on the store page. The metadata must be both search engine optimized and compliant with Google Play’s strict policies.

* **Program title (maximum 30 characters):**The title should be catchy, memorable, and descriptive (e.g., "Nova: AI Chatbot"). Developers should avoid using registered trademarks such as "ChatGPT," "OpenAI," or "Claude" in the app title or icon unless they have official written permission. Unauthorized use of these keywords is considered intellectual property infringement and will result in immediate removal of the app under our "Deceptive Behavior" policy.14.  
* **Short description (maximum 80 characters):**This section acts as the main hook and should quickly convey the bot's main value proposition to the user.  
* **Long description (maximum 4000 characters):**Google Play search algorithms rely heavily on this. Descriptions should include AI capabilities, use cases (e.g., translation, coding, text writing), and privacy guarantees. While Google’s current policies do not explicitly require disclosure of AI use in descriptions, international laws (such as the draft EU AI law) mandate transparency. Failure to mention the AI ​​nature of an app may be perceived as misleading, so including a clear disclaimer that the output is machine-generated and may contain errors is an essential step to build trust.14.  
* **Localization:**Translating the title, description, and keywords into different languages ​​(such as Persian, Arabic, Spanish, French) can significantly increase the discoverability of the app in global markets.

## **10\. Design and upload visual assets with the aim of increasing conversion rates**

Visual assets are the user’s first visual touchpoint with the app and have a direct impact on conversion rates (from visitor to installer). All of these assets must be designed and uploaded according to the exact specifications of Google Play.

* **App Icon:**It should be designed as 512x512 pixels in PNG or JPEG format (maximum 1 MB). The icon should be scalable and reflect the intelligent and interactive nature of the chatbot.  
* **Screenshots:**It is mandatory to submit a minimum of two and a maximum of eight screenshots for each device type (mobile, 7-inch tablet, 10-inch tablet). For a chatbot, these images should show the interactive user interface in action. Displaying complex questions and structured AI answers is very effective. It is very important that the dummy texts displayed in the screenshots do not contain any traces of sensitive, sexual, or otherwise illegal content, as screenshots are also scanned by the moderator bots.  
* **Feature Graphic:**This image is an advertising banner with dimensions of 1024x500 pixels that is displayed at the top of the store page and must have an attractive design that fits the brand.

## **11\. Completing mandatory data safety declarations (Data Safety Form)**

The Google Play Console has a crucial section called "Data Safety" that requires developers to be fully transparent about what data is collected from users, whether this data is shared with third parties, and what the app's security protocols are.30This information is displayed to users in the store as a privacy label.  
For AI chatbot applications, filling out this form requires extra attention to the backend architecture structure.26:

| Categorizing data in a form | Application in AI chatbot applications | Declaration status on Google Play |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Personal Information** | Email, name, user ID (to create an account). | If there is a registration system, it should be stated as data collected.30. |
| **Messages** | History of in-app chats, prompts, and user texts. | **Forced statement**This is the core of the robot's functionality and it should be noted that it is essential for the program to function.26. |
| **Data Sharing** | Sending prompts to external APIs (like OpenAI or Anthropic). | If the API provider retains data even for regulatory purposes (such as retaining 30 days of logs), this transfer must be recorded as a "sharing"22. |
| **Security Practices** | Encryption of data during transmission (Encryption in transit). | The developer must verify that all communications are encrypted via HTTPS/TLS.31. |
| **Data Deletion** | Ability to delete account and conversation history. | A direct link to the data deletion request form must be provided on the developer's website.19. |

The developer is solely responsible for the accuracy of this information, and any discrepancy between the actual behavior of the app and the statements in this form may result in the app being immediately removed from the store.26.

## **12\. Complete content, target audience, and age rating declarations**

Before an app can be submitted for final review, the developer must answer a series of questionnaires in the "App Content" section.

* **Content Rating:**The developer must complete a questionnaire provided by the International Age Rating Coalition (IARC). Since the chatbot displays user-generated content (and AI), the developer must carefully answer questions about filtering systems. If the chatbot has robust filters to block offensive language and sexual content (as required by Google policy), it may receive a lower age rating. Otherwise, the app will be classified as 18+.32.  
* **Target audience and family policies:**This section is extremely sensitive. The developer must choose the age range of the target audience for their app.**Very important warning:**If the app is designed to target people under 13, it must fully comply with Google Play's Families Policy.18. Generative AI programs will face much stricter oversight under this policy. The slightest error by AI in generating content inappropriate for children will result in a definitive blocking. Therefore, for public chatbots, it is strongly recommended that people over 18 years of age be selected as the target audience and that the description mention that the program is not suitable for children.15.

## **13\. Upload the final file, create a release version, and submit for review**

In this step, the developer combines all the collected components into a final version (Release).  
1\. In the Play Console menu, go to the section**Production**See. 2\. Click on the option**Create New Release**Click. 3\. Upload the compiled .aab file. Make sure this version is encrypted using valid keys (Google Play App Signing)344\. Write the Release Notes; provide a brief description of the features of this release and the AI ​​capabilities. 5\. Click**Review Release**Click. At this point, the console will automatically check and warn you of errors or missing information. 6\. Finally, click**Start Rollout to Production**Submit the application to the Review Queue.  
New AI-powered apps typically undergo a dual review process: first, automated scans for malicious code and unauthorized patterns, and then human moderation by the Google Play team.18Human moderators may interact with the chatbot to test the effectiveness of offensive content filters and the proper functioning of report buttons.18This process usually takes between 3 and 7 business days, but for complex AI applications it may take longer.

## **14\. Post-release management: Continuous monitoring of outputs and user reports**

The release of the app is not the end of the road, but the beginning of the crisis management and quality improvement phase. Users are always trying to bypass AI safety filters by using prompt engineering techniques (Prompt Injection and Jailbreaking) to produce prohibited content.  
The developer is required to actively monitor in-app user reports.17If reports indicate that users are receiving harmful or deceptive responses from AI, the developer should take action quickly. This response could include updating server-side system prompts, adjusting the language model temperature to reduce dangerous creations, and implementing stronger keyword blockers.16Failure to respond promptly to abusive reports is a direct violation of Google Play's content moderation policies and risks removal of the app from the store.16.

## **15\. Maintain long-term compliance with technical upgrades and policy updates**

The Google Play ecosystem and regulations around AI are evolving at an unprecedented pace. Google Play policies are typically updated quarterly, and developers should monitor the Developer Policy Center closely to stay informed of new changes to the generative AI rules.1.  
Additionally, annual technical updates are absolutely essential to maintain app visibility in the store. As mentioned earlier, upgrading the target API level of the app to newer levels (such as moving from API 35 to API 36 by August 2026\) prevents the app from being hidden from users of new phones.24Regularly releasing updates not only ensures security and legal compliance, but also signals to Google Play’s ranking algorithms that the chatbot is supported by an active and committed team, which will organically help improve the app’s ranking in search results and continuously increase its users.
